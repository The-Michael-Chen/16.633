Autonomy Stack Integration

Author

Dominic Maggio

1 Introduction

Finally, we now have enough tools in our toolbox to make our drones fly through the obstacles. We will spend this week getting our drone to fly though a single obstacle, then going forward we will fly though multiple obstacles.

2 Implementation

You already have a controller that can track april tags by trying to keep a target distance from a tag whose id number is inputted by the user and that tries to keep the tag in the center of the image. There will just be a few minor changes to get your drone to fly though an obstacle.

First, the drone should try to keep the center of the target point (the point under the april tag) centered in the image instead of using the center of the april tag. This point is determined by either knowing the location of the tag, or if that is not available, then use the point calculated from optical flow.

Second, instead of keeping a target distance from the tag, just set the target distance to 0 so the drone will fly through the tag. Note that if the tag is not in view, you won’t know how far you are from tag, so I could recommend just setting your drone to maintain some constant forward velocity if the tag is not in view.

One thing you may notice, is if your drone goes forward too fast, then it will not have time to correct it’s side to side motion and up down motion before it hits an obstacle. To fix this, consider reducing your forward velocity gain (or constant forward velocity value when the tag is not in view). Additionally, try adjusting the gains for the side to side controller and up-down controller.

3 Next Time

One piece that we are currently missing is how do we know when our drone has passed through the obstacle and hence is time to target a new obstacle. Next time, we will learn how to handle this. For now, your drone will just keep flying after it leaves the first obstacle so plan on pressing Ctrl-C to land your drone or at least make sure it has a safe location to crash :)
